Echo — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Echo (“Echo,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Echo mobile application (the “App”). Echo is a hyperlocal community app that lets you post and read messages and comments tied to your neighborhood.

1. Who we are

Echo is operated by Paul Leung. If you have any questions about this policy or your data, contact us at devechoapp@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

We only collect what the App needs to function. Specifically:

Account information

  • Email address — when you sign up or sign in with email/password or with Google Sign-In, to create and secure your account.
  • Authentication identifiers — a Firebase user ID and sign-in provider managed by Google Firebase Authentication.
  • You may also use the App as a guest (anonymous sign-in), in which case no email or profile is collected, but you cannot post, comment, or like.

Profile information (for registered accounts)

  • Your chosen username (@handle), first and last name, and an optional bio.

Content you create

  • The posts and comments you write, including any tags, the time of posting, and the likes you give.

Location information

  • With your permission, the App accesses your device's GPS location to: attach an approximate location to posts you choose to share, show how far away nearby posts are, and enforce a proximity rule for commenting on places of interest. Location is only collected when you grant the runtime permission and is used while the App is in use. You can post and browse without granting location access.

Technical and diagnostic information

  • Crash and stability data via Firebase Crashlytics (release versions only), to diagnose and fix crashes.
  • App integrity signals via Firebase App Check / Google Play Integrity, to verify requests come from the genuine, unmodified App and to prevent abuse.

We do not currently collect or upload photos or other files, and we do not sell your personal information.

3. How we use your information

  • Create and secure your account and keep you signed in.
  • Display your posts, comments, profile, and the neighborhood feed and map.
  • Show distance/proximity features for nearby content.
  • Diagnose crashes and protect the service from abuse and automated traffic.

4. How information is shared

  • Service providers (Google Firebase / Google Cloud). Your data is stored and processed using Google Firebase services (Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Crashlytics, App Check) on Google's infrastructure. See Google's privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy.
  • Google Maps Platform. The App uses the Google Maps SDK to display the map; your use of the map is subject to Google's privacy policy.
  • Other users. Your username, name, bio, and the posts/comments you create (and any location you choose to attach to a post) are visible to other users of the App.
  • Legal reasons. We may disclose information if required by law or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users or the service.

We do not share your data with third parties for advertising.

5. Data retention

We keep your account and content for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, your profile, username reservation, and account record are deleted as described in section 8. Diagnostic/crash data is retained per Google Firebase's standard retention periods.

6. Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and stored using Google Firebase's security infrastructure. Access to your account is protected by Firebase Authentication, and database access is restricted by server-side security rules.

7. Children's privacy

Echo is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Your rights and account deletion

You can access and update your profile information in the App at any time.

You can delete your account and associated data in two ways:

  1. In the App: open Settings → Account → Delete account. This re-verifies your identity and then deletes your profile, your username reservation, and your Firebase authentication account.
  2. On the web: if you cannot use the App, request deletion at our account-deletion page (or by emailing devechoapp@gmail.com). We will verify your identity and delete your account and associated data.

Depending on your location, you may have additional rights (access, correction, deletion, portability). To exercise them, contact devechoapp@gmail.com.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, notify you in the App.
